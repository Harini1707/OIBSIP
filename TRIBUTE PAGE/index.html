A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong), Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). In 1969 he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India. Rejoining DRDO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname “Missile Man.” Among those successes was Agni, India’s first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.

Early Life and Education

  • 1.Early Education:Abdul Kalam completed his early education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram, Tamil Nadu. He was an average student but showed a keen interest in mathematics and science.
  • 2.Graduation: Kalam pursued his undergraduate studies at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, affiliated with the University of Madras. He graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Physics in 1954.
  • 3.Aeronautical Engineering: After completing his graduation, Kalam aspired to become a fighter pilot in the Indian Air Force but did not qualify. However, he was determined to pursue a career in aeronautical engineering.
  • 4.Madras Institute of Technology: In 1955, Kalam gained admission to the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in Chennai, where he pursued a degree in Aeronautical Engineering. He was part of the first batch of students to enroll in the Aerospace Engineering program at MIT.
  • 5.Mentorship and Research: While at MIT, Kalam was mentored by renowned scientist Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, the father of the Indian space program. Under Sarabhai's guidance, Kalam actively participated in research and gained valuable insights into rocketry and space science.

ABDUL KALAM AS A SCIENTIST

STARTED HIS CARRIER AS SCIENTIST IN 1960

  • After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (by Press Information Bureau, Government of India) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS). He started his career by designing a small hovercraft, but remained unconvinced by his choice of a job at DRDO.
  • Kalam was also part of the INCOSPAR committee working under Vikram Sarabhai, the renowned space scientist.
  • In 1969, Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980; Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO in 1965.In 1969, Kalam received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers.
  • In 1963 to 1964, he visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility.Between the 1970s and 1990s, Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.

    • Death

  • Former President APJ Abdul Kalam, also known as the Missile Man of India, passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong on July 27, 2015.

  • He suffered a cardiac arrest while delivering a speech at IIM Shillong and collapsed, leading to his death.

  • Soon after his collapse, he was rushed to a hospital in the city where doctors declared him dead. It was reported that he had suffered from a cardiac arrest.

  • Abdul Kalam died on July 27, 2015 (07:45 Indian Standard Time GMT+5:30) at Bethany Hospital

  • Thousands including Prime Minister Narendra Modi attended the funeral held in his hometown of Rameshwaram.
